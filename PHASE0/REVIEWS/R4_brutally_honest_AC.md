# Brutally Honest ICML AC — Final Decision Review

## Verdict
WEAK REJECT (leaning BORDERLINE on a generous panel)

## Acceptance probability at SCALE (0-100)
**28/100.** A workshop with ~25-30% acceptance has room for null-result audits, but this one fights two headwinds: (a) the null is on a single vendor with N=15 per probe cell, and (b) the headline "mitigation" is unvalidated by construction. On a panel that values methodological discipline, it lands around the 25-35th percentile. On a panel that explicitly courts negative results (some SCALE chairs do), it could squeak in at ~40. I'd bet against acceptance, but not heavily.

## What would push this from current to ACCEPT (in order of impact)
1. **Add at least one non-Anthropic vendor.** A second vendor (OpenAI gpt-4o-mini or gpt-5-mini, or Gemini 2.5 Flash) at the *same* probe scale would convert the headline from "Anthropic-2026 doesn't fabricate" to "frontier-tier 2026 models don't fabricate." That is a categorically different and far more defensible claim. Cost: ~$22 again. The paper already justifies the omission as a budget constraint, but the reviewer pool will read that as the load-bearing weakness. This is the single highest-leverage change.
2. **Add an open-source weaker tier with non-zero TEHR.** Run Qwen3-8B (already in the harness per §4.1) or Llama-3.1-8B on the same probe. If TEHR>0 there, the paper's regime-dependence claim becomes empirically grounded rather than speculative, and RVR gets a non-empty subset to validate against. This converts "designed-but-unvalidated mitigation" into "validated on the regime it targets, null on the regime it doesn't." That's a real contribution.
3. **Run a registry-design probe that actually breaks the model.** Construct a registry where the only available tools are non-decomposable for the task (no filesystem-class redundancy), and the target is removed. The current miss_func split fails to elicit fabrication because BFCL gives the agent five workaround tools; the paper concedes this in §1 but never tests the obvious counterfactual. A targeted "no-decomposition-path" registry would either elicit hallucination (validating RVR) or strengthen the null (deepening the surprise).
4. **Tighten the MetaTool comparison or drop it.** The 25%→0% framing is the paper's most quotable hook. If the protocol isn't tightly matched (and §1 says "analogous," which is hedge language), reviewers will hammer this. Either reproduce MetaTool's exact protocol on Sonnet/Haiku to get a clean head-to-head 25%-vs-0% delta, or downgrade the framing to a qualitative reference and stop leaning on the number.
5. **Increase N on the probe.** N=15 per cell with cluster-bootstrap CIs gives [0.0, 0.0] only because the point estimate is exactly zero; a single fabrication event would explode the upper CI. The paper currently dodges this by reporting "0.0%" without showing the CI width on the probe table — that's a 95% upper bound near 20% per cell at N=15. At least one reviewer will notice. Triple to N=45.

## Top-5 bad points (specific, actionable)
1. **[BLOCKER] — Single-vendor evaluation undermines the headline claim.** The abstract and §1 generalize to "modern frontier models"; the data covers only Claude Sonnet 4.6 + Haiku 4.5. Either narrow the claim throughout to "Anthropic 2026 frontier+small tier" (boring but defensible) or add a second vendor (~$22). The §7 generalization disclaimer is honest but doesn't repair the headline.
2. **[BLOCKER] — RVR is unvalidated and the paper knows it.** §5.4 explicitly says "RVR's recovery effect can only be measured on a non-empty subset … that subset is empty." Releasing harness code is fine, but listing RVR as contribution (iii) in §1 with the implication it's a real method is misleading to a casual reader. Either reframe RVR as "design + harness for future replication" (demoting it from contribution to artifact), or delete the four-condition ladder discussion from §3.3 entirely — currently it occupies ~40% of the methods section for a method that produces no result.
3. **[MAJOR] — Probe N=15 with [0.0, 0.0] CIs is statistically thin.** Cluster-bootstrap on 15 tasks where every observation is zero produces a degenerate CI; the per-cell 95% upper bound on a Wilson interval is roughly 20%. Table 2 should report the upper CI bound, not "0.0%". This is the single easiest reviewer ammunition target.
4. **[MAJOR] — The Czapla setup is not actually reproduced.** Czapla's phenomenon is ambient runtime functions colliding with declared registries; BFCL has no ambient functions. §1 and §7.7 acknowledge this but the paper still calls the probe a "Czapla-style scenario" — which it isn't. The probe is "target-removed-plus-distractor," a different and weaker setup. Either rename the probe to drop the Czapla framing, or build a registry with simulated ambient reachability (a separate executor with extra tools that the registry omits but that resolve when called) — that's the actual reproduction of Czapla and would be the paper's best shot at a non-zero TEHR.
5. **[MINOR] — Citation hygiene problems will get noticed.** §2 has TODO arXiv-ID flags visible in source comments (`bugstudy2026`, `licl2026`, `fissiongrpo2026`, `englander2026`). At least one reviewer will check these and find them unverified. Resolve before submission or remove the citations.

## Surprise score for the null result (0-100)
**42/100.** Low-medium-surprising. Anyone who's used Claude 4.5+ for tool use has noticed it doesn't fabricate names on well-formed registries; the BFCL TEHR=0 finding is "expected with mild interest." The genuinely surprising piece is the *miss_func split* result — BFCL specifically designed that split to elicit hallucination and it doesn't. That deserves more emphasis. The probe result (0% across distractor types with target removed) is more surprising at face value but the redundant-tool-basket explanation in §1 deflates it: of course the model picks `cd`+`ls`+`mv` over a near-name distractor when those are sitting right there. To push surprise above 60 you'd need: a registry without decomposition paths, or a vendor mix, or non-zero MetaTool replication on the same models.

## Most damaging weakness in 1 sentence
The paper's three contributions reduce to "we measured zero on one vendor's two models, and we built but cannot test a mitigation," which is structurally one observation plus one piece of unvalidated infrastructure — thin for a workshop that explicitly wants methodological depth.

## Most defensible strength in 1 sentence
The pre-registered analysis plan (§4.4 with 16 locked decisions, asymmetric-falsifiability protocol for M-Constrain, dual gap-closure ratios) is more rigorous than 80% of submissions to this workshop and would survive any methods-stickler review.

## Honest 2-paragraph review I would actually submit

This paper measures the Tool-Existence Hallucination Rate across six regime tests on Anthropic Sonnet 4.6 and Haiku 4.5 and reports TEHR=0/1571 across all conditions. The pre-registration is exemplary, the harness is well-engineered, and the trace inspection convincingly shows decomposition rather than fabrication as the dominant failure mode on miss_func splits. As a null-result audit, the methodological scaffolding is among the most disciplined I've seen at this workshop. The miss_func TEHR=0 is the genuinely interesting finding — BFCL designed that split specifically to elicit fabrication, and Sonnet 4.6 chains five smaller in-registry calls instead. That observation alone is worth a workshop slot if framed honestly.

The submission has three problems that I cannot get past at the current acceptance rate. First, the headline generalizes to "modern frontier models" on data from a single vendor's two models; the §7 disclaimer is a fig leaf for a claim the abstract makes much more strongly. A second vendor at the same probe scale would cost ~$22 and would meaningfully change the paper's standing. Second, RVR is listed as contribution (iii) but is, by the authors' own §5.4 admission, untestable in this evaluation envelope. Releasing harness code for replication is fine, but presenting an unvalidated mitigation as a method-paper contribution is overreach — it reads as a design artifact dressed up to make the null feel less hollow. Third, the probe's N=15 cells produce CIs that are degenerate (0.0,0.0) only because every observation is zero; the actual upper bound at N=15 is ~20%, which the tables hide. Fix one of these and the paper is a clear weak accept; fix two and it's a clear accept. As is, my recommendation is weak reject — the work deserves to exist and the harness is a legitimate community contribution, but the paper as written is a regime-test of one vendor with an unvalidated method, and SCALE has stronger submissions to fund this year.
