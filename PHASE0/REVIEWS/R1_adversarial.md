# Adversarial Reviewer — Round 1

## Persona
Tired senior reviewer, sixth submission of the day, allergic to renamed metrics and marketing prose; will reject on the first unbacked claim.

## Verdict
**WEAK REJECT.** This is a denominator change wrapped in a system-prompt trick, padded with a ratio that masquerades as a scaling law. With targeted patches (see below) it could climb to WEAK ACCEPT; nothing in the current draft justifies more.

## Lines of attack

1. **TEHR is API-Bank's "API Hallucination" with a different denominator.** The authors' own prior-art audit concedes "~80% overlap with TEHR. The remaining 20% is the *denominator*." The §2 differentiation reduces to: "TEHR... isolates the registry-membership dimension at the per-tool-call denominator." That is not a new metric; that is a normalization choice. Nothing in §3.1 describes a *behavior* TEHR detects that API Hallucination misses. The contribution is reframed as "disaggregation," which is a verb, not a result. Either show a case where per-call vs. per-task changes a qualitative conclusion, or stop pretending this is contribution #1.

2. **RVR is the membership check every production framework already does, plus a copy-paste of the registry.** OpenAI's tool API rejects unknown names. Anthropic's does. The novelty is reduced to "we put the list of allowed names back into the conversation on rejection." §3.2(i) literally says "Echoing $\mathcal{R}$ into the conversation places every valid name in the model's recent context, exploiting the autoregressive prior." That is a system-prompt trick dressed in citation clothing. The paper claims this "[achieves] the same effect as token-level constrained decoding without requiring logit access" (§3.2(iv)) — except constrained decoding *guarantees* membership; RVR samples and hopes. False equivalence.

3. **"Cost-quality-gap closure" is marketing language built on a fragile ratio.** §2.3 contribution #4 and §4.3 define gap-closure as `[PassRate(small+C1) − PassRate(small+C0)] / [PassRate(frontier+C0) − PassRate(small+C0)]`. The denominator is a small difference between two noisy quantities; on tasks where small ≈ frontier or where small > frontier (it happens), the denominator goes to zero or flips sign and the headline ratio explodes or inverts. There is no mention of clipping, winsorizing, or stratification. The "headline" will be whatever bootstrap subset the authors pick. Worse: the framing assumes the gap is *due to* hallucinations — the paper never decomposes the small-vs-frontier gap into hallucination-induced vs. other failure modes. Without that decomposition the closure ratio is a coincidence of magnitudes, not a causal story.

4. **Five models, conveniently the ones the authors had credits for.** §3 of the plan: "OpenAI $2,500 + Anthropic $500 + xAI $2,500." The model lineup follows the wallet, not the design. No Gemini (no Google credits), no Llama (no inference budget), no o3/o4 (cost). The paper claims "5–6 models spanning 3 capability tiers and 2–3 vendor families" as if this were principled coverage. Two vendors plus a maybe-Grok is not vendor diversity; it is two vendors. The "tier" assignment (Sonnet 4.6 = frontier, Haiku 4.5 = small) is also vendor-internal marketing, not a measured capability axis.

5. **The local tier is a vanity panel.** §4 setup: "The local tier is included contingent on a Phase-0 feasibility check; its results are reported in a separate *consumer-hardware feasibility* panel and are not pooled with the API tiers." Pre-registered decision #12: same. So it does not affect the headline gap-closure number, does not affect the per-tier statistical tests (Holm-Bonferroni is across API tiers), and does not feed the scaling-curve claim. Yet the abstract uses it to claim "open-source 8B local model" coverage and the SCALE pitch leans on it ("directly addresses SCALE's 'efficient agents' theme," PAPER_PLAN §2.4). Either pool it and accept the consequences, or drop it and stop cashing the rhetorical check.

6. **The mechanism probe is statistically too thin to support a causal claim.** §3.4: "30 BFCL tasks ... three distractor types." That is 30 × 3 = 90 observations per model — and per-cell it is 30. The plan promises "ANOVA"-grade conclusions about which surface features drive hallucination. With 30 obs/cell and almost certainly low base rates, type-II error swamps everything; any null result is uninterpretable and any positive result is borderline. The authors then call this "a causal — not merely correlational — claim" (§3.4 last sentence). It is not. It is a small, underpowered manipulation study.

7. **Czapla is an industry blog and is doing too much load-bearing work.** §2 quotes Czapla as the empirical anchor for the phenomenon ("Claude 4.5 hallucinated access to a tool I hadn't given it yet"). It is cited in the abstract motivation, §2.4 of the plan, and the contribution defense. A blog post posted three months before submission cannot be the empirical foundation of the problem statement when peer-reviewed precedents (API-Bank's 61.4% rate, RelyToolBench, the Zhu et al. bug study) exist. Demote Czapla to a footnote; lead with Li et al. and Cao et al.

8. **At-most-one retry is asserted, never justified or ablated.** §3.2(ii): "Bounding to a single re-prompt caps token and latency overhead, matches typical production retry budgets, and prevents pathological loops." Three handwaves in one sentence. Where is the sensitivity analysis on $k \in \{0, 1, 2, 3\}$? $k=0$ *is* C0 by definition, so the paper does include that boundary, but the sweep over $k>1$ is missing. If $k=2$ recovers another 10pp, the cap is wrong. If $k=2$ recovers nothing, the cap deserves a footnote, not a paragraph of post-hoc rationale.

9. **"Training-free deployment behind any black-box API" is not what the paper actually demonstrates.** §3.2(iii) and the differentiation paragraph (§2 last block) both claim deployability. The paper instruments BFCL and τ-bench — academic harnesses with synthetic registries. Production deployments use OpenAI's tool API, which already returns a structured rejection on unknown names; the user-facing question is whether RVR materially beats *that*, not whether it beats the harness's "framework default error string" (§3.3 C0). The paper does not run a production-API condition where the SDK's own rejection error is the C0; it runs a condition the authors built. Strawman C0.

10. **The C0/C0.5/C1 contrast is what the paper *should* be about, but the writing buries it.** The single useful design choice — "C1 vs C0.5 isolates the *content* of the re-prompt" (§3.3, §2 final paragraph) — is buried under the gap-closure marketing. Lead with this. The honest paper here is "naive retry vs. registry-augmented retry, paired McNemar, here is the delta." That is a 4-page workshop note. The authors are stretching it to 7 pages with a probe (underpowered, attack 6), a local tier (excluded from the headline, attack 5), a gap-closure ratio (unstable, attack 3), and a vendor-coverage claim (wallet-shaped, attack 4).

11. **Power claim is internally inconsistent.** Pre-reg decision #10: "Power target is $\Delta\mathrm{Pass} \geq 20$pp at 80% power; if hallucination events per cell fall below 30, framing is downgraded to exploratory." So the headline rests on $\geq 30$ hallucination events per cell. With $N=50$ BFCL tasks and a pilot TEHR target of "$\geq 5\%$ on $\geq 2$ tiers" (Gate G2), the strict subset on which McNemar is computed could easily be $\leq 10$ tasks per model. The 20pp effect target then requires an effect so large that anything smaller will be invisible, and anything visible will be on a sample so small that confidence intervals will be uninformative.

## What would change my mind
1. **Decompose the small-vs-frontier gap.** Show, with held-out data, that hallucination-induced failures account for $\geq X\%$ of the small-tier deficit on these tasks. Without that, the "gap closure" ratio is an aesthetic, not an argument.
2. **Run a real production-API C0.** Use OpenAI's and Anthropic's native tool rejection error as C0 (not a harness wrapper). If RVR still beats *that* C0, the deployability claim survives.
3. **Sweep retry budget and report the curve.** $k \in \{0,1,2,3\}$ on at least the pilot subset. Either justify $k=1$ as the elbow or rewrite §3.2(ii).

## Most likely meta-review outcome
AC will read attack #1 (TEHR ≈ API-Bank), attack #3 (gap-closure ratio instability), and attack #6 (probe is underpowered) and conclude the paper is a workshop-tier engineering note dressed as an ICML contribution. With the patches above it lands at borderline; without them, reject.
