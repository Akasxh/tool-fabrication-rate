On the matched Qwen3 sweep RVR drives $20\!\to\!0$ (Fisher one-sided
$p\!=\!3.5\!\times\!10^{-5}$; \S\ref{sec:results:rvr}); $\mathrm{C}_{0.7}$ alone

the prompt (\S\ref{sec:results:controls}). Hence the executor allow-list is
load-bearing, not optional. Not a quantization artefact: persists at bf16
(\S\ref{sec:results:controls}). \\

\textbf{Audit first} (run the $\mathrm{C}_0$ target-removed probe), then deploy

a larger model is \emph{not} safer by default, so capability is no substitute

does not prove safety. The executor allow-list is the guarantee; RVR/$\mathrm{C}_{0.7}$
reduces the load reaching it. \\
