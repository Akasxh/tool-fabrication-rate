\paragraph{The zero-event frontier is broad; the non-zero band is open-weight.}
Across two commercial vendors and eleven model versions, the
target-removed distractor probe elicits \emph{zero} fabrication events.
The five OpenAI tiers (gpt-4.1, gpt-4.1-mini, gpt-4.1-nano, gpt-4o,
gpt-4o-mini) pool to $0/2{,}117$ parsed calls (Clopper--Pearson $95\%$
upper bound $\leq\!0.14\%$), and the five Anthropic~4.x versions pool to
$0/2{,}578$ under $\mathrm{C}_0$ ($\leq\!0.12\%$;
Section~\ref{sec:results:probe}). Every open-weight model with a non-zero
rate is in the Qwen3 family: $0.95\%$ at $1.7$B, $1.33\%$ at $4$B,
$1.49\%$ at $8$B, $1.87\%$ at $14$B (Table~\ref{tab:frontier}), with the
$0.6$B and $32$B endpoints at $0\%$. The split is clean enough to
\emph{state} but not clean enough to \emph{attribute}: the commercial
models are reached through each vendor's structured function-calling API,
whereas the open-weight models are self-hosted $4$-bit MLX builds whose
calls we recover by text-parsing the generated stream. A structured API
that refuses or drops a malformed call removes fabrication surface that a
text-parse path still sees, so we cannot separate the model from its
serving path. We report the contrast as a serving-and-model bundle, not a
pure model property, and we draw no scaling law or cross-vendor capability
ranking from it (Section~\ref{sec:limitations}).
